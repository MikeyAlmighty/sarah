#+title: Least Common Multiple (LCM)

The **Least Common Multiple (LCM)** of two or more integers is the **smallest positive integer** that is a multiple of each of the given numbers.

\[
\text{LCM}(a, b) = \min \{n \in \mathbb{N} \mid a \mid n \ \land\ b \mid n\}
\]

---

**Examples:**
- \( \text{LCM}(4, 6) = 12 \)
- \( \text{LCM}(5, 7) = 35 \)
- \( \text{LCM}(12, 15) = 60 \)

---

**Methods of Finding LCM:**

1. **Prime Factorization Method:**
   - Factor each number into primes.
   - Take the **highest powers** of all primes involved.
   - Multiply them.

   Example:
   \[
   \begin{align*}
   12 &= 2^2 \cdot 3 \\
   15 &= 3 \cdot 5 \\
   \text{LCM}(12, 15) &= 2^2 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 = 60
   \end{align*}
   \]

2. **Using GCD:**
   \[
   \text{LCM}(a, b) = \frac{|a \cdot b|}{\text{GCD}(a, b)}
   \]
