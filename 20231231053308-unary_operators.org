* Unary Operators
*Unary* operator is one that requires exactly *one* ~operand~, or ~variable~, to function.

A *unary* operator has only one operand ([[id:9bcf2aef-060a-4011-98b5-cb949a9f7d1a][binary operator]] has two).

For example, the =–= operator in ~–5~ is a *unary* operator to negate number ~5~, whereas the =–= operator in ~4 – 5~ is a [[id:9bcf2aef-060a-4011-98b5-cb949a9f7d1a][binary operator]] for *subtracting* ~5~ from ~4~.
