Asymmetric Encryption

Works well in untrusted environments but more computationally expensive and complex

aka Public-Key ciphers

Uses different keys to encrypt and decrypt

A process that uses a pair of related keys – one public key and one private key – to encrypt and decrypt a message and protect it from unauthorized access or use.

Very important part of Asymmetric Cyptography is Public Key Signature

1. Asymmetric Cyptography Allows:

  1. Confidentiality (Granted through encryption/decryption mechanism)
  2. Authenticity (Digintal Signature mechanism, message wasn't tampered with)
  3. Non-repudiation (Came from person claiming to be author)
