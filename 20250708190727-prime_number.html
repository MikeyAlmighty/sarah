Prime Number

Prime numbers are natural numbers greater than 1 that have exactly two distinct positive divisors: 1 and the number itself.

Key Characteristics:

  • Indivisible by any number other than 1 and itself.
  • The first prime number is 2, which is also the only even prime.
  • Used extensively in number theory, cryptography, and computer science.

Examples:

  • Primes: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19…
  • Not Primes: 1 (only one divisor), 4 (divisible by 2), 9 (divisible by 3)
